## STRATEGIC FRAMEWORK: "VALUE-FIRST POSITIONING"

**Core Insight:** Security company owners don't buy software. They buy:
1. **Time back** (to focus on growth, not admin)
2. **Peace of mind** (compliance, reliability)
3. **Profit margins** (cost optimization)
4. **Professional image** (impress clients with tech sophistication)

**Current Problem:** Landing page talks about features ("AI Roster Generator"). Buyers don't care about HOW. They care about RESULTS.

### ğŸ§  Pricing Psychology Applied

#### 1. **Anchoring Effect**

**Principle:** First number seen becomes reference point for value judgment

**Application:**
- Show Enterprise (highest) first? NO - buried at end
- Show annual pricing BEFORE monthly? YES - makes monthly seem cheaper
- Lead with SAVINGS amount (R15K/month) before subscription cost (R499)

**Example Reframe:**

âŒ **Bad:** "R1,299 per month"
âœ… **Good:** "Save R40,000/month for just R1,299"

The R40K anchors perception â†’ R1,299 seems tiny

#### 2. **Decoy Effect (Asymmetric Dominance)**

**Principle:** Adding a third option makes the target option seem better

**Application in Our Tiers:**

```
Starter: R499 (30 guards)
Professional: R1,299 (100 guards) â† TARGET (most want this)
Business: R2,999 (300 guards) â† DECOY (makes Professional seem like bargain)
```

**Buyer Psychology:**
- "R2,999 is too much for me..."
- "But R1,299 gets me almost everything for half price!"
- "And R499 is too limiting (only 30 guards)"
- **Decision: Professional** âœ“

#### 3. **Loss Aversion Framing**

**Principle:** Avoiding a loss is more motivating than acquiring a gain

**Application:**

âœ… **Loss Frame (Stronger):**
- "Stop losing R15K/month to inefficient scheduling"
- "Avoid R50K+ PSIRA fines"
- "Don't waste another 8 hours this week"

âŒ **Gain Frame (Weaker):**
- "Save R15K/month"
- "Earn R50K back"
- "Gain 8 hours"

**Science:** Loss aversion is 2-2.5x stronger than gain attraction (Kahneman & Tversky)

#### 4. **Price Perception: Charm Pricing**

**Should we use R499 or R500? R1,299 or R1,300?**

**Research:**
- R499 vs. R500: Minimal difference for B2B (charm pricing is consumer psychology)
- R1,299 vs. R1,300: Actually, round numbers signal "premium" in B2B

**Recommendation:**
- Starter: R499 (small enough that charm pricing works)
- Professional: R1,300 (round = premium)
- Business: R3,000 (round = serious enterprise purchase)

**Typical SaaS Discount:** 15-20% for annual prepayment
